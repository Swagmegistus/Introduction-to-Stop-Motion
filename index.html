Stop Motion

What is Stop Animation?

Stop animation, also known as stop-motion animation, is a filmmaking technique where objects are physically manipulated in small increments between individually photographed frames. This creates the illusion of movement when the frames are played in sequence. It's a very time-consuming process that has been used in various forms of media, including films, television shows, and even games.

How Stop Animation Works?

In stop animation, objects or characters are moved slightly between each frame of the animation. The movement can be done physically or digitally. These frames are captured using a camera and assembled on a software such as DaVinci Resolve. Examples of Stop Animation

Some famous examples of stop animation include:

  • Wallace and Gromit
  • The Nightmare Before Christmas
  • Coraline
  • Chicken Run
  • Shaun the Sheep

In summary.

The first stop motion animation was "The Humpty Dumpty Circus" produced in 1898. Although stop motion animation fell out of favour due to the introduction of sophisticated digital media like SFX and more realistic animation, recently, stop motion animation is experiencing a resurgence due to its stylistic choice and charm. To sum up stop motion animation is a great artform that blends the digital with the tactile and physical. A lot of patience is needed to create good animation.
